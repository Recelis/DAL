This is the equation for solving for `Eigenvectors`.

I is the `Identity Matrix`, which is a matrix which multiplied by another matrix equals that other matrix with no change.

$$(\mathbf{A} - \lambda\mathbf{I}) = 0$$

But the matrix needs to be `singular` and `non-invertible`, meaning the `determinant` of the matrix must be 0.

$$\det(\mathbf{A} - \lambda\mathbf{I}) = 0$$

**Where:**

- $\mathbf{A}$ is the original matrix.
- $\mathbf{I}$ is the Identity Matrix.
- $\lambda$ is the unknown eigenvalue we are solving for.
- $\det(\cdot)$ is the determinant function.
